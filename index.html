RICE REUNION HISTORY

James Rice

James Rice, Sr. first appears in any official document in August of 1812 when his Ohio Militia unit was activated for the War of 1812. He later served another tour in Joseph Ross’s unit in September 1813. These two tours later earned him 160 acres of Land Bounty land in Iowa for his service (80 acres for each tour).
James Rice, Sr
Ohio Residence Background: James and Violetta Rice purchase their Ohio farm on 18 November 1834 in Athens County, Ohio. They sold the farm on 1 September 1856. Due to realignments in county boundary lines the farm now lay in adjoining Madison Township, Vinton County, Ohio when they sold the farm. In 1854, they sold a right away through their property to the Marietta and Cincinnati Railroad Company for construction of the railroad. Their farm was close to Zaleski, Ohio, a village laid out in 1856 in Vinton County, Ohio. The population was 278 at the 2010 Federal Census.
